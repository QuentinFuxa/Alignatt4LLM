\captionof{figure}{Three coordination regimes for ASR and MT sharing one GPU. The submitted system uses the strict ASR/MT alternation in~(c). In full async mode~(a), successive MT launches need not correspond to comparable source-time increments: a long MT decode can absorb several incoming ASR updates, while the next relaunch may cover much less newly available audio. That makes history-dependent policies such as local agreement \citep{liu20s_interspeech} less natural to calibrate there than an attention-only policy such as AlignAtt, which scores each draft directly against the currently accessible source prefix.}
